Kasinadhuni Viswanath (Telugu: కాశీనాధుని విశ్వనాధ్; 19 February 1930 – 2 February 2023), popularly known as "Kalatapasvi", was an Indian film director, screenwriter, lyricist and actor. One of the greatest auteurs of Telugu cinema,[1][2][3] he received international recognition for his works, and is known for blending parallel cinema with mainstream cinema.[a] He was honored with the "Prize of the Public" at the "Besançon Film Festival of France" in 1981.[6] In 1992, he received the Andhra Pradesh state Raghupathi Venkaiah Award, and the civilian honour Padma Shri for his contribution to the field of arts.
